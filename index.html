Wake Up;
Do Something

Your guide to thinking straight and acting real when the world gets weird.

  1. 1
    Confirmation bias means you’ll believe something is true just because you already think it is - not because it is. Watch how you Google.
  2. 2
    The news cycle is optimized for engagement, not accuracy. That means outrage, fear, and conflict - not context or solutions.
  3. 3
    Social media shortens your attention span and raises your anxiety. The more you use it, the worse you’ll think the world is.
  4. 4
    Dopamine is about anticipation, not satisfaction. That’s why you keep scrolling even when it’s not fun.
  5. 5
    The brain doesn’t finish developing until about 25 - especially the part that resists impulse. Be patient with your poor past self.
  6. 6
    People mirror emotions. If you're calm, they calm. If you're angry, they escalate. You choose the feedback loop.
  7. 7
    The sunk cost fallacy keeps people in bad jobs, relationships, and habits. Just because you’ve invested time doesn’t mean you should keep going.
  8. 8
    Sleep deprivation makes you impulsive and emotionally irrational. Don’t try to “power through” - you’re becoming dumber and more reactive.
  9. 9
    Trauma can be passed epigenetically - not just culturally. Your parents' or grandparents' stress can shape your physiology.
  10. 10
    Most of your behavior is unconscious. You rationalize after the fact. Learn to watch what you do, not what you say you believe.
  11. 11
    The availability heuristic makes rare events feel common. Seeing one plane crash doesn’t make flying dangerous.
  12. 12
    Corporations use psychological tricks to keep you hooked. Infinite scroll, red badges, “limited time” offers - it’s behavioural design.
  13. 13
    Multitasking makes you worse at everything. Task-switching is not free - it burns cognitive fuel and degrades memory.
  14. 14
    Your gut affects your mind. An unhealthy microbiome can increase anxiety and depression.
  15. 15
    Exercise is a proven antidepressant. Regular movement is more effective than pills for many people.
  16. 16
    Groupthink happens fast. If everyone in a room agrees too easily, something important isn’t being said.
  17. 17
    The placebo effect is real. Belief changes physiology. Use it ethically, not cynically.
  18. 18
    Cognitive dissonance makes people double down when shown they’re wrong. Don’t argue to win - argue to open.
  19. 19
    No one is paying as much attention to you as you think. This is liberating - stop performing.
  20. 20
    You are not a fixed identity. The self is fluid, context-dependent, and largely constructed after the fact.
  21. 21
    AI doesn’t understand - it predicts. It’s guessing the next likely word, not thinking.
  22. 22
    The Dunning-Kruger effect means the most confident people are often the least competent. Be skeptical of loud certainty.
  23. 23
    Capitalism doesn’t reward what’s meaningful - it rewards what’s profitable. This is why many valuable jobs are underpaid.
  24. 24
    People don't act based on facts; they act based on stories that feel true. Understand narrative, not just logic.
  25. 25
    The “Just World Fallacy” makes people blame victims. The world isn’t fair - bad things happen to good people.
  26. 26
    Your phone steals your presence. Even when it’s in your pocket, it reduces your working memory.
  27. 27
    Mindfulness isn’t about peace - it’s about awareness. You won’t always feel better, but you’ll see more clearly.
  28. 28
    Depression isn't always sadness - it's often flatness, numbness, or irritation. Learn the signs early.
  29. 29
    Repression doesn’t erase feelings - it buries them until they leak out elsewhere. Usually at the worst time.
  30. 30
    Your nervous system learns safety or danger from your relationships. Regulate with people, not just alone.
  31. 31
    The body holds memory. Trauma often appears as pain, tightness, shutdown - not just thoughts.
  32. 32
    You can have compassion and still set boundaries. Letting people walk over you isn’t kindness - it’s fear.
  33. 33
    People don’t change from shame. They change when they feel safe enough to see clearly.
  34. 34
    Every social media post is curated. You’re comparing your behind-the-scenes to someone’s highlight reel.
  35. 35
    FOMO is a manipulation tool. You’re not missing out - you’re being sold a feeling.
  36. 36
    Psychotropic drugs don't "fix" you - they create new baselines. Sometimes useful, sometimes numbing. Be informed.
  37. 37
    Silence is powerful. Most people rush to fill it because they’re uncomfortable with themselves.
  38. 38
    Most goals are just proxies for emotion. You don’t want a six-pack - you want to feel respected or in control.
  39. 39
    Your attention is the most valuable thing you own. Guard it like your life depends on it - because it does.
  40. 40
    Desire is manufactured. Ads and influencers are designed to make you feel a lack.
  41. 41
    People can’t think clearly when they’re scared. Fear hijacks the brain’s executive function. Start with safety.
  42. 42
    Procrastination is often perfectionism in disguise. You're afraid of doing something “bad,” so you don’t do it at all.
  43. 43
    Solitude is not loneliness. Learn to be with yourself, or you’ll seek company that fills the void poorly.
  44. 44
    “Should” is usually someone else’s voice in your head. Replace it with “do I want to” or “is this true?”
  45. 45
    We evolved to survive, not to be happy. But we can train for meaning, connection, and resilience.
  46. 46
    Most modern suffering is symbolic. You’re not running from tigers - you’re ruminating about an email.
  47. 47
    Consumerism sells identity. You're not buying a product - you're buying a story about who you are.
  48. 48
    When in doubt, breathe. Long, slow exhales calm your nervous system. It’s biology, not woo.
  49. 49
    It’s okay not to know. Doubt is the beginning of wisdom, not the absence of it.
  50. 50
    You will die. Memento mori. Use it to focus on what matters.
  51. 51
    Your brain rewards prediction, not truth. It prefers a wrong but familiar answer to an unfamiliar but correct one.
  52. 52
    Most people don’t listen - they wait to speak. Learn to actually listen and you’ll understand far more than others do.
  53. 53
    “Overthinking” is often unprocessed emotion disguised as logic. Get out of your head by feeling what's beneath the thoughts.
  54. 54
    The phrase “that’s just how I am” is usually a defense, not a fact. Personality isn’t fixed.
  55. 55
    "Toxic positivity" dismisses pain in the name of cheerfulness. Sometimes things are just hard, and that's okay to admit.
  56. 56
    The average adult attention span is now under 8 seconds. This is a technological problem, not a moral failing.
  57. 57
    You can feel two things at once. Grief and gratitude. Anger and love. Complexity is human.
  58. 58
    Being busy is often a way to avoid yourself. Stillness reveals what you’ve been running from.
  59. 59
    Comparison is self-harm dressed as ambition. Measure yourself by your values, not other people’s highlight reels.
  60. 60
    The body can't distinguish between real and imagined threat. That anxious text you haven’t replied to? Same cortisol as danger.
  61. 61
    Shame is about who you are; guilt is about what you did. One is paralyzing, the other is useful.
  62. 62
    People tend to remember how you made them feel, not what you said. Especially in conflict.
  63. 63
    Reassurance often reinforces anxiety. Learn to sit with discomfort instead of escaping it.
  64. 64
    Saying “I’m fine” when you're not doesn't build strength - it builds distance.
  65. 65
    Wealth is not the same as freedom. And freedom is not the same as peace.
  66. 66
    You can learn a lot about someone by how they handle “no.” Rejection reveals character.
  67. 67
    Discomfort is the price of growth. If you want change, expect awkwardness, tension, and emotional friction.
  68. 68
    Saying less is often more powerful. Stillness in speech is clarity in mind.
  69. 69
    Most apologies fail because they’re self-protective. A real apology names the harm and expects nothing in return.
  70. 70
    You’ll never be fully ready. If you wait until you are, you’ll never move.
  71. 71
    Friendship in adulthood takes intentional effort. Don’t wait to “feel like it” - schedule connection.
  72. 72
    You don’t need to monetize every passion. Not everything has to be a hustle.
  73. 73
    Strong opinions are often hiding insecurity. Certainty can be a defence mechanism.
  74. 74
    Long-term relationships are built on repair, not perfection. Learn to fight well.
  75. 75
    Modern food hijacks your biology. It’s not just about willpower - it’s by design.
  76. 76
    You can’t think your way out of a body problem. Sometimes the answer is breath, sleep, movement - not insight.
  77. 77
    “Tired but wired” is a sign of nervous system dysregulation. You’re not lazy - you’re overstimulated.
  78. 78
    Goals without systems are fantasies. Build habits, not heroic plans.
  79. 79
    Most feedback is projection. Take what's useful. Leave the rest.
  80. 80
    If your life feels empty, it might be full of the wrong things.
  81. 81
    "Self-care" isn’t bubble baths - it’s doing the uncomfortable stuff that keeps you grounded long-term.
  82. 82
    Labels can help or trap. Use them for understanding, not identity cages.
  83. 83
    Silencing your needs isn’t maturity - it’s learned helplessness.
  84. 84
    Your nervous system is shaped by your childhood environment. But it’s not fixed.
  85. 85
    Most people are terrified of real intimacy. Vulnerability feels like danger to a brain wired for survival.
  86. 86
    You’re allowed to grieve dreams that no longer fit. Letting go is not failure.
  87. 87
    A regulated nervous system feels boring at first if you’re used to chaos. Stability isn’t apathy.
  88. 88
    Not everything is trauma - but everything affects you. Validating your experience doesn’t require dramatizing it.
  89. 89
    Doing something poorly is better than doing nothing. You learn by trying, not by waiting for perfection.
  90. 90
    The “self” is a process, not a thing. There’s no core “you” - just patterns, habits, and stories you can rewrite.
  91. 91
    We live in a meaning crisis. That doesn’t mean nothing matters - it means you have to choose what matters.
  92. 92
    If you don’t define success, someone else will. Probably an algorithm or a marketing team.
  93. 93
    Cynicism is lazy self-protection. It’s fear wearing the mask of intelligence.
  94. 94
    Beauty isn’t shallow. Aesthetics can be grounding, healing, and profound. Curate your space with intention.
  95. 95
    Technology outpaced culture. Your brain is using ancient tools in a digital war zone.
  96. 96
    Learning to say “I don’t know” might be the most underrated skill of all.
  97. 97
    Art is necessary. Not because it’s productive, but because it speaks when nothing else can.
  98. 98
    Meaning doesn’t arrive. It’s made, moment by moment, in how you show up.
  99. 99
    You are not behind. The timeline is a myth. The game is rigged. Live anyway.
  100. 100
    Survival is not enough. Pay attention. Wake up. Participate.
  101. 101
    Challenge authority, but know that genuine expertise matters. An expert’s hard-won knowledge outweighs your Google search or Facebook thread-especially in their own field.
  102. 102
    Flexibility beats certainty. The world changes fast. Being adaptive is a superpower-clinging to fixed views or plans is a fast track to irrelevance.
